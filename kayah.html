Karenni (Kayah) State

Karenni State, Burma

        The Karenni, also known as the Red Karen (-ni meaning red referring to their preferred colour of clothing) are a subset of the Karen people. Covering around 9 different ethnic subsets, the karenni are estimated to number about 300,000 people. Together with the Mon, they are the oldest indigenous group in Burma, Migrating from China in the 6th or 7th century. Karenni (or Kayah) state sits between Karen and Shan state along Burma's border with Thailand.
        Karenni state was independent until the British colonization of Burma in 1886. In 1948, Karenni state was incorporated into the newly independent Burma without the acknowledgment or consent of the Karenni people. After over 60 years of fighting, the Karenni army signed a ceasefire with the Burmese army in March 2012. Although a similar ceasefire agreement was signed in 1995, it collapsed only three months later.
